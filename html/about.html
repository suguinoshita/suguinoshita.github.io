About BeamSectionCalc

   BeamSectionCalc is a free online tool developed by engineers to engineers - or whoever else it may be useful!
   Here you can find a fast, clear and reliable platform not only to calculate a variety of cross-section properties, but also to compare different options side-to-side! Our goal is to help you to make the best decisions always.

Calculate and compare cross-sections

Compare different designs and choose the best solution!
Start by selecting one or more cross-sections to be computed - for instance, if you want to compare two or more options for your project.

In the plots, you can verify which option is lighter, stiffer, or will show lower stresses under the same loads (assuming simple traction load or simple bending moment on each axis, and same beam lengths and homogeneous material).

You can change the dimensions or section types at anytime and all the results will be updated instantly! You may also choose which section is to be used as reference in the plots!

Practical example

Let's assume you are designing a beam for some component in you project. Also, in this example, you must select between a range of circular tubes and you know that the main loads are bending moments. You did your calculations, and you found that a section with diameter 25.4mm and 3mm thickness will suit your needs.

But how can you improve your design?
Using our tools, as in the image below, you can find that another circular tube, but with diameter 31.8mm and thickness 1.6mm is 28% lighter, 28% stiffer, and shows 2.8% lower bending stresses (of course, under simplifying assumptions, as same material and boundary conditions).

Of course, this is just one example and there are many more possibilities you could try and adjust to the specific requirements of your projects!

Sensitivity Analysis

Visualize how each dimension affects the beam properties and adjust your design efficiently!

You may ask yourself: which dimension should be modified to lower the stresses in an efficient way?
or
which dimension can I change to reduce mass, but with minimal impact in the bending stiffness?

This page can help you!

Start by selecting one cross-sections to be computed.
In the plots, you can verify which dimension has the higher (or lower) effect on the beam mass, stiffness and stresses!
*again, assuming simple traction load or simple bending moment on each axis, and same beam lengths and homogeneous material.

Complete set of equations

All the equations are available to help your insights!

Here you can find the centroid coordinates, cross-section area, outer perimeter, second moments of area (used on bending stiffness and bending stress calculations) and polar moment of inertia (used for torsion loads). The elastic modulus are also shown, which can be used to estimate and compare bending stresses for each cross-section.
